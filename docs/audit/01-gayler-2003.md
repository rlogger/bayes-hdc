## Summary (200 words)

**Claim.** A "little-known family of connectionist models" Gayler labels *Vector Symbolic Architectures* (VSAs) — descendants of Smolensky's tensor-product binding that avoid its exponential dimensionality blow-up — provably answer all four challenges Jackendoff (2002) levelled at neural cognition: (1) the massiveness of the binding problem, (2) the problem of 2 (multiple instances of one type), (3) the problem of variables / productivity, and (4) functional equivalence of working memory and long-term memory.

**Algorithms / equations.** VSAs share a triple of operators on fixed-dimensional vectors: a *multiplication-like* binding, an *addition-like* bundling/superposition, and a *permutation-like* protection operator. For concreteness Gayler uses MAP (Multiply-Add-Permute) coding: `bind(a,b) = a*b` (elementwise product), `unbind(cue, trace) = bind(inverse(cue), trace)`, with self-inverse binding `a*a = 1`. Frames of role-filler pairs are formed via `make-frame(a) = bind(P(a), a)`, and substitution is `apply-substitution(make-substitution(x,b), bind(x,y)) = b*y`.

**Terminology.** This paper introduces the term "Vector Symbolic Architecture" itself ("the name 'Vector Symbolic Architectures' has been invented to cover a family of related approaches").

**Demonstrated tasks.** Variable binding, role-filler frames, productive substitution, recursive structure construction (`a*b*c*d = (a*b)*(c*d)`), star-attribute binding for the example sentence "The little star's beside a big star".

**Successor citations.** Cited 325× (Semantic Scholar). It is the canonical etymology citation in every modern VSA/HDC review (Schlegel et al. 2022; Kleyko et al. 2023).

## Substantive findings (for user review)

1. **[medium] No `make_frame` / `make_substitution` primitive matches Gayler's frame & substitution algebra.** Gayler's response to Challenge 2 (Problem of 2) introduces `make-frame(a) = bind(P(a), a)` to give frame instances unique identity tokens, and his response to Challenge 3 (Productivity) treats substitution as a binding of two structures. Neither is exposed as a public primitive in `bayes_hdc/__init__.py`. This is reasonable — they are macros over `bind`/`permute` — but if the library is positioning itself as the canonical VSA algebra in JAX, a one-line `make_frame(x, vsa)` helper would let users replicate the Gayler-2003 examples verbatim, and a docstring example would tie the public API to the originating paper.
   - File:line: `bayes_hdc/vsa.py` (no current location)
   - Proposed action: discuss whether to add `frame(x, vsa)` and `apply_substitution(sub, struct, vsa)` as opt-in helpers, or document the one-liner `vsa.bind(vsa.permute(x), x)` recipe in `examples/kanerva_example.py` with explicit attribution.

2. **[low] DESIGN.md §1 lists VSA laws but does not motivate VSA against the connectionist alternative.** The library's algebraic-laws section (`DESIGN.md:14–25`) reads cleanly as "here are the axioms" but never says *why* an HDC user should care about an algebra at all. The Gayler-2003 framing — that backprop-trained MLPs are demonstrably unable to handle massive binding, productivity, and the working/long-term-memory transparency that linguistic cognition requires — is precisely the rationale that justifies the rest of the design. A short subsection ("Why an algebra at all? — Jackendoff's four challenges") with one paragraph per challenge and a forward-reference to the relevant module would tighten the motivation. This is editorial, not algorithmic.
   - File:line: `DESIGN.md` between current §1 and §2
   - Proposed action: optional design-note subsection; not blocking.

3. **[low] `examples/kanerva_example.py` ("Dollar of Mexico") is a Gayler/Kanerva-style demonstration but the Gayler 2003 framing is not surfaced.** The Kanerva analogy example is the textbook role-filler frame, exactly the construction Gayler walks through in his Response 2 with `frame-1 * (is-a*star + size*little)`. Naming Gayler 2003 as a co-citation in that example's header docstring would let readers see the lineage (Smolensky → Plate/Kanerva → Gayler's unifying VSA framing → this library).
   - File:line: `examples/kanerva_example.py` (header docstring)
   - Proposed action: one-line addition to the example's docstring.
